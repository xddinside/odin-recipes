Ramen

Description

Ramen is a comforting Japanese noodle soup known for its deeply flavorful broth, chewy wheat noodles, and a variety of savory toppings. The broth can be made from chicken, pork, or vegetables and is often seasoned with soy sauce, miso, or salt. Aromatics like garlic, ginger, and mushrooms are simmered to build umami, while vegetables such as bok choy, carrots, or kale add freshness and crunch. Classic toppings include soft-boiled eggs, sliced pork belly, green onions, nori (seaweed), and chili oil. Ramen is highly customizable, allowing for endless combinations of broths, noodles, and toppings, making each bowl a unique and satisfying meal

Ingredients

  • Egg & Oils
    • 1 large egg
    • 1 ½ teaspoons spicy sesame oil
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided

  • Pork & Seeds
    • 2 (1x3-inch) boneless pork chop slices
    • 1 tablespoon black sesame seeds, divided

  • Herbs
    • 2 leaves fresh basil, chopped, divided
    • 1 leaf fresh sage, chopped, divided

  • Noodles & Seaweed
    • 1 (3 ounce) package instant ramen noodles (such as Shirakiku®), seasoning packet discarded
    • 3 tablespoons shredded dried kombu

  • Soup Base & Seasoning
    • 1 ¾ tablespoons bonito soup stock (such as Hondashi®)
    • 1 ¾ tablespoons soy sauce (shoyu)
    • ½ teaspoon white miso paste with dashi

Steps

  1. Prepare the egg:
    • Fill a small pot halfway with water. Bring to a boil.
    • Add egg and cook for 15 minutes. Drain.
    • Run cold water over egg and set aside to cool.

  2. Cook the pork:
    • Pour sesame oil into a large skillet. Lay pork slices in oil.
    • Cover pork with 1 tablespoon olive oil, ½ tablespoon sesame seeds, ½ of the basil, and ½ of the sage.
    • Flip pork and repeat with remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil, ½ tablespoon sesame seeds, basil, and sage.
    • Cook covered over medium-low heat for 5 minutes per side, until browned. Remove from heat.

  3. Cook the noodles:
    • Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
    • Cook ramen noodles for 3 minutes until tender. Drain and rinse.
    • Return noodles to the pot.

  4. Prepare the broth:
    • Stir kombu, bonito stock, soy sauce, and miso paste into noodles.
    • Cook over medium heat, stirring with chopsticks, until miso dissolves (3-5 minutes). Transfer to a serving bowl.

  5. Assemble and garnish:
    • Peel and halve the boiled egg lengthwise.
    • Arrange egg, pork slices, and fish paste slices over noodles.
    • Garnish with tonkatsu sauce, ginger, green onion, and black pepper.
